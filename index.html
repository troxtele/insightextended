Overview

Our Portfolio Management Tool is designed to provide you with a comprehensive solution to track and manage your market decisions over time. With this tool, you will have the ability to monitor your portfolio's performance and evaluate how you are doing compared to a selected market.

One of the key features of the Portfolio Management Tool is the ability to track the performance of your portfolio on a daily basis. By monitoring the unit price of your assets, you can easily assess the progress of your investments and make informed decisions based on real-time updates

The tool also offers full action logs, allowing you to keep a record of all your activities such as adding or withdrawing funds, adding or removing positions, and updating positions. This feature ensures transparency and accountability, enabling you to review your actions and make adjustments as needed.

Additionally, the Portfolio Management Tool provides a comprehensive view of the return rate for all your assets, including long positions, short positions, and cash positions. You can compare your portfolio's performance against benchmarks, giving you valuable insights into your investment strategy and overall market trends.

Getting Started

Setting up your portfolio and configuring the necessary settings is the first step towards effectively managing your investments. In this "Getting Started" section, we will walk you through the process of getting your portfolio up and running.

1. Adding Funds

To begin, you need to add funds to your portfolio. Click on the "$" sign to add funds to your portfolio. You can enter the amount of funds you want to add to your portfolio. You would be able to use this option in future to make adjustments to your portfolio's capital based on changes in your investment strategy or financial needs. All fund transactions, including additions and withdrawals, are logged and presented in the fund history for your reference and record keeping.

The amount you add will be translated into the initial unit price, which is set at 1 USD by default. This initial unit price serves as a reference point for tracking the performance of your investments. (!) Note: when recording the first deposit in the fund history audit log, ensure that the date of the deposit is earlier than the date of your first opened positions. This chronological order is important to accurately reflect the sequence of events in your portfolio.

2. Adding Positions

Once you have funds in your portfolio, you can start adding, removing, and updating positions. Adding a position involves specifying the symbol or name of the asset, the quantity or amount invested, and the price at which it was acquired. This information allows the tool to accurately calculate the performance of each position.

By clicking the "+" button, you can manually add positions to your portfolio. This feature allows you to enter specific investments you own, such as stocks, or ETFs, along with their corresponding quantity and purchase price.

If you prefer that Tipigo would find and suggest investment opportunities, we recommend using the Opportunity Scanner tool, located under the "Tools" section. The Opportunity Scanner uses advanced algorithms to analyze analyst and market data and identify potential investment opportunities that match your criteria.

Please note that by default, the system is not directly connected to a trading service. Therefore, in order to maintain accurate portfolio numbers, you will need to manually enter the exact figures from your trading system or brokerage account. This ensures that your portfolio reflects your actual holdings and provides accurate performance calculations.

This Add Position screen allows you to specify the type of investment, whether it's a buy (long) or sell (short) position. You can enter symbols for stocks or ETFs, and once accepted, they will be displayed in green.

For each position, enter the symbol, quantity, and purchase price. The system will automatically pull the market value based on the symbol entered. However, it's important to adjust the market value according to the actual figures from your trading system or brokerage account. This ensures that your portfolio reflects accurate and up-to-date information.

Portfolio Monitor

The portfolio monitor is a robust tool designed to provide you with in-depth insights into your investment portfolio. By offering near real-time updates and a wide range of customizable options, the portfolio monitor offers a holistic view of your investments, empowering you to stay informed about your portfolio's performance and make data-driven investment choices. In this section, we will walk you through the diverse functionalities and capabilities of the portfolio monitor, enabling you to unlock its full potential and optimize your investment portfolio effectively.

Portfolio Summery

The Daily P&L (Profit and Loss) provides you with a measure of the daily gains or losses in your portfolio's value. It represents the change in the value of your portfolio on a day-to-day basis, helping you track the performance and fluctuations of your investments over time.

The Sharpe Ratio is a metric that assesses the risk-adjusted return of your portfolio. It measures the excess return generated by your portfolio compared to a risk-free investment, relative to the volatility of the portfolio. A higher Sharpe Ratio indicates a better risk-adjusted performance, as it signifies higher returns for the amount of risk taken.

The Sharpe Ratio calculations are based on the performance of your portfolio over a three-month period. They provide valuable insights into risk characteristics of your investments, allowing you to evaluate and analyze the performance of your portfolio.

  1. 1. ASSETS UNDER MANAGEMENT - providing you with total P&L in USD and %
  2. 2. TOTAL LONG POSITIONS - providing you with long (buy) positions P&L in USD and %
  3. 3. TOTAL SHORT POSITIONS - providing you with short (sell) positions P&L in USD and %
  4. 4. TOTAL CASH - providing you with total available cash and unit price

Positions Table

    he investment table provides a comprehensive overview of your investments, allowing you to track important details, monitor performance, and make informed decisions about your portfolio.

    Here's an explanation of each column in the portfolio table:

  • Symbol: This column displays the symbol or ticker of the investment, representing a specific stock or ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund).
  • Type: This column indicates the type of investment, such as "Buy" or "Sell." It helps you identify the nature of the position in your portfolio.
  • Company: This column provides the name of the company associated with the investment, helping you easily identify the underlying company behind the symbol.
  • Sector: This column indicates the sector to which the company belongs based on the system-selected data provider. It categorizes investments based on their industry or sector classification.
  • Entry Date: This column displays the date on which the investment was added to your portfolio, allowing you to track the timing of your investment decisions. Entry
  • Price: This column shows the price at which the investment was purchased or entered into your portfolio.
  • Quantity: This column represents the number of shares or units of the investment held in your portfolio.
  • Last Price: This column displays the latest price of the investment, providing the current market value.
  • Value: This column shows the total value of the investment, calculated by multiplying the quantity by the last price. It represents the current worth of the investment in your portfolio.
  • P&L (Profit and Loss): This column displays the profit or loss generated by the investment, indicating the difference between the current value and the initial investment cost.
  • Earning Date: This column shows the count of upcoming earning dates for the investment.
  • Rel Weight (Relative Weight): This column indicates the relative weight of the investment in your portfolio, representing its proportion compared to the total portfolio value.
  • P&L (%): This column displays the percentage profit or loss generated by the investment, indicating the percentage change in value compared to the initial investment cost.
  • Benchmark P&L (%): This column shows the percentage profit or loss of the investment compared to a benchmark index ETF, enabling you to compare its performance.
  • Daily Change: This column indicates the percentage change in the investment's price or value on a near real-time basis, reflecting the fluctuations in its performance.

Notification Types:
  • Sell - this is a rating from the analyst team. Getting that notification means that if you have that symbol as a long position you should sell it as soon as possible. For Sell position do nothing.
  • uy - this is a rating from the analyst team. Getting that notification means that if you have that symbol as a long do nothing. for Sell position you should Buy as soon as possible.

Other Monitoring Events

Symbol reached target price:
This event occurs when the price of a symbol reaches its predefined target price. It serves as a notification that the symbol has achieved the desired price level, which could be a signal for potential actions such as selling the position or reassessing the investment strategy.

ymbol price below MA50:
When the price of a symbol falls below its 50-day moving average (MA50), this event is triggered. It indicates that the current price is lower than the average price over the past 50 days. This event may suggest a potential downturn or weakening trend, prompting investors to monitor the symbol closely for further analysis or potential adjustments to their portfolio.

Symbol price below MA100:
Similar to the previous event, this monitoring event occurs when the price of a symbol drops below its 100-day moving average (MA100). It indicates that the current price is lower than the average price observed over the past 100 days. This event may signal a more significant shift in the symbol's trend or potential long-term weakness, warranting further attention and analysis.

Symbol earning date nearing:
This event notifies users when the earning date for a symbol is approaching. The earning date refers to the scheduled date on which a company will release its financial results, including revenue, earnings, and other key metrics. Monitoring this event allows investors to stay informed about upcoming earnings announcements, which can impact the market perception and potentially influence the price and performance of the symbol. Being aware of the earning date enables investors to plan their actions and evaluate the potential implications on their investment decisions.

These monitoring events serve as valuable indicators and notifications within the portfolio management tool, helping users stay informed about important market and symbol-specific events to make timely and informed investment decisions.

Close Positions

To close a specific investment position in your portfolio, you can utilize the "Close Position" function. Please follow these steps:

  1. Locate the position you wish to close within the portfolio management section. You can find the action menu represented by three dots, typically located at the right end of the position table row.
  2. Click on the three-dot action menu corresponding to the position you want to close.
  3. From the options presented, select the "Close Position" option.
  4. Before proceeding, it's important to note that the portfolio management tool is not directly connected to a trading system. Therefore, the actual closing of the position should be done on your trading system or platform first.
  5. On your trading system, execute the necessary steps to close the position and obtain the exact closing price.
  6. Return to the portfolio management tool and enter the precise closing price you obtained from your trading system. Update the position with the exact closing price and any additional details required.
  7. If you intend to close only a portion of the position, specify the quantity or percentage you wish to close.
  8. It is recommended to utilize the notes feature in the portfolio management tool to document the reason for closing the position. This serves as a decision log and can provide valuable insights and context for future reference.

Please remember that closing a position involves selling or liquidating the investment, thereby removing it from your portfolio. It is crucial to carefully evaluate and consider the decision to close a position, taking into account your investment strategy, objectives, and market conditions. By using the "Close Position" function, you can effectively manage your portfolio and track the performance of your investments. Remember to update the portfolio with the exact closing price obtained from your trading system to maintain accurate records and performance metrics

"Delete Position" option:

The "Delete Position" option allows you to remove a specific investment position from your portfolio. Please follow these steps to delete a position:

  1. Locate the position you want to delete within the portfolio management section.
  2. Look for the action menu represented by three dots, typically found at the right end of the position table row.
  3. Click on the three-dot action menu corresponding to the position you wish to delete. From the options presented, select the "Delete Position" or "Remove Position" option.
  4. Confirm the deletion when prompted to ensure the removal of the position from your portfolio. It's important to note a few considerations:
    • Deleting a position permanently removes it from your portfolio, and the associated data and historical information will be lost. Exercise caution when deleting a position, as this action cannot be undone.
    • Before proceeding with the deletion, ensure that you have carefully reviewed the position and have made a deliberate decision to remove it from your portfolio.
      The "Delete Position" option is typically used when you want to eliminate a particular investment from your portfolio's records.
    • If you accidentally delete a position or need to retain historical data, it is recommended to consult with your support or administrator for guidance.
    • By utilizing the "Delete Position" option, you can maintain an accurate and up-to-date record of your portfolio, removing any unwanted or irrelevant positions. However, it's essential to exercise caution and consider the long-term implications before deleting a position from your portfolio.
"Edit Position" option:

The "Edit Position" option allows you to modify the details of a specific investment position within your portfolio. Follow these steps to edit a position:

  1. Locate the position you want to edit within the portfolio management section.
  2. Look for the action menu represented by three dots, typically found at the right end of the position table row.
  3. Click on the three-dot action menu corresponding to the position you wish to edit.
  4. From the options presented, select the "Edit Position" or "Modify Position" option.
  5. A dialog box or editable form will appear, allowing you to make changes to various parameters of the position.

    Here are some details you can typically edit:

  • Entry Date: Modify the entry date of the position if you need to reflect the accurate date of when the investment was initiated.
  • Entry Price: Update the entry price to reflect any adjustments or corrections in the initial purchase price.
  • Quantity: Modify the quantity of shares or units held in the position.
  • Notes: Add or modify any notes or comments related to the position for better record keeping and decision-making.

    It's important to note a few considerations:

  • The ability to edit a position may depend on the specific features and capabilities of your portfolio.
  • The changes made through the "Edit Position" option typically reflect updates within the portfolio management tool itself. Ensure that any corresponding changes or adjustments are also made in your trading system or other sources of record to maintain accurate data.
  • Review your edits carefully before confirming, as any modifications made will affect the position's historical data, calculations, and overall portfolio analysis.
  • In some cases, there may be restrictions on editing certain fields or parameters. If you encounter any limitations, reach out to your portfolio management tool's support or administrator for further guidance.
  • By utilising the "Edit Position" option, you can keep your portfolio information up to date and accurate. It allows you to make necessary adjustments and corrections to reflect the most accurate information regarding your investment positions.
